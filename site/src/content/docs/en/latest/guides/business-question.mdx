title: Frame a business question
description: Turn a decision into a reviewable analysis request before the agent starts.
---

## Frame the decision and question

A useful analysis starts with the decision it will support, not with an available table or
column. Complete this worksheet before asking the agent to analyze data:

```text
Decision: What decision will this analysis support?
Question: What do you need to know?
Metric: Which business metric, as approved by its business owner, represents the outcome?
Scope: Which population and time window are included or excluded?
Comparison: What baseline makes the result meaningful?
Breakdowns: Which dimensions are valid and useful?
```

If you cannot fill in every field, say what is known and leave the uncertain field explicit.
Do not substitute a physical column name for a business-owned metric definition.

## Ask the agent to propose an analysis

Send the completed worksheet with instructions like these:

> Use the `marivo-analysis` skill to turn this worksheet into an analysis proposal. If the
> business decision or another required choice is missing, ask me one clarification at a
> time and wait for my answer. Browse the semantic catalog and read the relevant object
> details before proposing the analysis. Never infer a metric's meaning from column names
> or sample values. Show me the authored definitions and guardrails, propose a metric,
> scope, comparison, and useful breakdowns, then stop and ask me to decide or approve their
> business meaning and use before analysis starts. Do not change or add semantic objects
> during this step.

Catalog details expose the definitions and guardrails that authors recorded; they do not
grant business approval. The agent should use them to rule out unsupported proposals and
show the remaining choices to the business owner. If the worksheet requires a missing or
technically unready semantic object, the agent should name the gap and route it to the
`marivo-semantic` skill for drafting or technical validation instead of inventing a
substitute. The business owner still decides or approves the metric's meaning and the
proposed scope, comparison, and breakdowns.

## Review and approve the proposal

Before analysis starts, explicitly confirm all four of these choices:

- **Metric:** the selected metric's authored definition expresses the business outcome you
  meant, and you approve that meaning for this decision.
- **Scope:** the population, time window, exclusions, and grain match the decision.
- **Comparison:** the baseline is appropriate and makes the result interpretable.
- **Breakdowns:** every proposed dimension complies with the authored guardrails, is valid
  for the metric, and is one you approve as useful for the question.

Ask the agent to revise the proposal if any choice is ambiguous. Once all four are confirmed,
continue with [First agent-guided analysis](/en/latest/first-analysis/) and require scoped
readiness before the first observation. Readiness verifies the technical semantic handoff;
it does not approve any business choice.
