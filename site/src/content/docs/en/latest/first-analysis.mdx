title: First agent-guided analysis
description: Give an agent a business question, then review the analysis and its evidence.
---

## Before you ask the agent

Start with a Marivo project whose business meaning you have approved and whose semantic
objects are technically ready for analysis. If you
do not yet have one, follow the [Quick Start](/en/latest/quick-start/) or ask an agent to use
the `marivo-semantic` skill to draft and technically validate the semantic layer. You remain
responsible for deciding or approving its business meaning before analysis begins.

You do not need to write Python or choose operators yourself. Your job is to state the
business question, name the decision it supports, and review the agent's proposed scope.

## What you need to decide

For a useful first analysis, decide:

- which business decision the answer will inform;
- which metric, with business meaning you approve, represents the outcome;
- the population and time windows to include or exclude;
- the baseline that makes the change meaningful; and
- which breakdowns you approve as meaningful and whose authored guardrails permit the use.

If any of these are unclear, use the [Frame a business question](/en/latest/guides/business-question/)
worksheet before starting.

## What to ask the agent to do

Give the agent a concrete request like this:

> Use the `marivo-analysis` skill to investigate why Q4 revenue decreased.
>
> Use the sales.revenue metric whose business meaning I approved. Compare 2026 Q4 with 2025 Q4 by month,
> and inspect region as a possible driver. Read each metric and dimension's
> authored business definition and guardrails before analysis. Stop if technical readiness is
> blocked, the requested breakdown conflicts with those guardrails, or I have not approved
> its business use. Show each material result,
> then give me an answer-first summary with evidence, caveats, and unresolved
> questions. Do not change the semantic layer during this analysis.

Replace the metric, periods, and possible driver with the choices relevant to your
decision. Keep the instruction to stop on technical-readiness, guardrail, or business-
approval problems. A semantic gap should return to semantic authoring for drafting or
technical validation instead of silently changing definitions; you must still decide or
approve its business meaning.

## What the agent will do

You should be able to follow the analysis as a visible sequence:

1. The agent browses the catalog and reads the selected metric, time dimension, and
   breakdown details, including their authored business definitions and guardrails. These
   details inform the proposal; they do not grant business approval.
2. The agent runs a scoped readiness check for exactly those objects and stops if the
   handoff is blocked.
3. The agent runs `observe` for the current and baseline periods and shows each material
   result before choosing the next step.
4. The agent runs `compare` to quantify the change.
5. If the breakdown complies with authored guardrails and you approved its business use,
   the agent runs `attribute` and
   inspects the resulting evidence.
6. The agent closes with an answer-first, evidence-backed recap rather than only a table
   or raw output.

`catalog` details, scoped readiness, `observe`, `compare`, and `attribute` are Python
surfaces the agent runs. They are not commands you need to type.
Scoped readiness only verifies the technical semantic handoff. It does not decide whether
a metric meaning, scope, comparison, or breakdown is right for the business question.

## What to review before continuing

Before accepting the conclusion or asking for a deeper drill-down, confirm that:

- the metric definition matches the business outcome you meant;
- the current and baseline periods, monthly grain, population, and exclusions are right;
- region complies with the authored guardrails and is a breakdown you approve as meaningful
  for this metric;
- every material claim points to an inspected Marivo result or evidence record;
- caveats, quality limits, blockers, and unresolved questions are explicit; and
- the agent did not change the semantic layer to make the requested analysis fit.

If a metric, dimension, relationship, or definition is missing, stop the analysis and use
the `marivo-semantic` skill to draft or technically validate that named gap. Review and
approve its business meaning before continuing; technical readiness is not business
approval.
